# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two overlapping audiences visiting the public marketing site:

1. **Technical decision-makers** (CTOs, VP Eng, eng leads) — evaluating whether Dust fits their stack, security posture, and integration needs. Skeptical, fast readers, skip to the proof.
2. **Business operators** (ops, revenue, marketing, CS leads) — looking for concrete evidence that AI agents work for their team's specific workflow. Motivated by outcomes and peer adoption, not architecture.

Both arrive with a low trust budget. They want to see results, not promises.

## Product Purpose

Dust is a platform for building and operating AI agents that work with a company's actual knowledge and tools. It targets organizations that have moved past "experimenting with AI" and want to deploy agents that non-technical teams can build and own. Success looks like operators across functions shipping agents that save measurable time, without involving engineering for every change.

## Brand Personality

Precise, confident, grounded. The voice is that of a practitioner talking to other practitioners — no hype, no hand-waving. Outcomes over features. The brand earns trust by showing, not telling.

## Anti-references

- **HubSpot**: noisy, illustration-heavy, over-friendly. Dust is not trying to be approachable at the cost of credibility.
- **Generic SaaS cream-and-purple**: the Notion/Webflow/Linear clone aesthetic — soft gradients, purple accent, rounded sans, floating UI screenshots on beige. Dust's palette is cooler and its register is more serious.
- **AI hype decks**: glowing orbs, neural network meshes, "the future of work" headlines. Dust is already in production at real companies; it doesn't need to sell a vision.

## Design Principles

1. **Show proof, not promise.** Every section should answer "does this actually work?" with a name, a number, or a real workflow — not a generic benefit claim.
2. **Respect the reader's time.** Hierarchy is ruthless. The most important claim is the biggest thing on the screen. Redundancy is cut, not softened.
3. **Precision over warmth.** Dust is a professional tool. The design should feel like something built for people who ship things, not for people who need to be reassured.
4. **Earn the enterprise signal.** Security, permissions, and compliance are not footnotes — they're a primary reason enterprise buyers choose Dust over building their own. Surface them early and specifically.
5. **Operators are the hero.** The product enables human operators to build and run agents. The narrative centers the person doing the work, not the AI itself.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No specific WCAG level mandated. Reduced motion handled via `prefers-reduced-motion` throughout. No known color blindness constraints beyond standard contrast ratios.
